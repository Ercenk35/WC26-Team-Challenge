How to Play & the Rules

FIFA World Cup 2026 · Team Challenge

The goal

Predict the outcome of every FIFA World Cup 2026 match. The sharper your predictions, the more points you collect. Climb the office leaderboard and earn the bragging rights.

Getting started

Enter your name (or a nickname) once on the home screen — that name is your identity for the whole tournament, so use the same one every time you return. No password needed.

Making a prediction

For each match you do two things:

  1. Pick the winner — tap the trophy button above a team's flag, or tap Draw if you think it ends level.
  2. Predict the score — set how many goals each team scores (0–10) in the two boxes.

Then hit SUBMIT PREDICTION. You can change your pick as many times as you like up until kickoff. All kickoff times are shown in Turkey time (GMT+3). The moment a match kicks off it locks 🔒 and can no longer be changed.

How points are scored

Points are awarded per match and added together:

  • Correct winner — you picked the right team to win, or correctly called a draw: +10
  • Exact score — you got both teams' goals exactly right: +5
  • One team's goals — the exact score is wrong, but you nailed one team's goal count: +1

The +5 (exact score) and +1 (one team) bonuses are never combined — you receive whichever one applies.

Worked example

Suppose you predicted: Türkiye to win, with a score of England 1 – 2 Türkiye. Here is how different final results would score:

The Leaderboard

Everyone's points are totalled and ranked so you can see exactly where you stand against your colleagues. Points appear once a match's final score has been entered.

The Predictions Board

See what everyone predicted for each match — who they backed to win and their score guess — along with the points each person earned once results are in. All predictions are public, so play fair.
